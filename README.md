# Seminar: Laserfusionsversuch am LLNL ⚛️

## About the Project
This repository contains the presentation slides for a short seminar on the laser fusion experiments at the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (LLNL), focusing on the National Ignition Facility (NIF). 

The seminar was held as a mandatory qualification prerequisite (*Zulassungsvoraussetzung*) for the final module exam in **"Kerne und Teilchen"** (Nuclear and Particle Physics) within the Physics B.Sc. program at the **Friedrich Schiller University Jena**.

> **Note:** The presentation slides (`Laserfusionsversuch_Trinity.pdf`) and the talk itself are in German, while the repository documentation is maintained in English.

## Key Topics & Content
The presentation provides a compact physical and technical overview of the historic breakthrough in inertial confinement fusion (ICF):
* **The LLNL & NIF Infrastructure:** Technical setup of the world's largest laser facility.
* **The DT Fusion Reaction:** Energetics of the ${}^2\text{H} + {}^3\text{H} \rightarrow {}^4\text{He} + \text{n}$ reaction, releasing $17.6 \text{ MeV}$ of total energy.
* **Inertial Confinement Fusion (ICF):** Mechanism of indirect drive inside the *Hohlraum*, X-ray conversion, and spherical shockwave compression.
* **Energy Balance & Breakthrough:** Analysis of the historic December 2022 shot ($E_{\text{laser}} = 2.05 \text{ MJ} \rightarrow E_{\text{fusion}} = 3.15 \text{ MJ}$, achieving a target gain $Q_{\text{target}} \approx 1.54$).
* **Engineering Challenges:** Critical discussion on repetition rates, mass-production of targets, and tritium breeding.

## Technical Q&A: Energy Extraction & Reactor Infrastructure
A crucial question often raised during the seminar is how the generated fusion energy is technically extracted and whether it has already been fed into the power grid. According to official documentation from the LLNL:

> *"The Target Chamber with its utility systems provides the vacuum environment needed during the experiment, holds the final optics assemblies that focus the laser light on the target, and contains debris and x rays generated during experiments. The chamber, a sphere 10 meters in diameter, was assembled from 10-centimeter-thick aluminum panels that were pre-formed and then welded in place. It is covered with 0.3 meters of concrete that has been injected with boron to absorb neutrons from fusion reactions. The remaining neutrons are absorbed by the concrete Target Bay and switchyard walls to protect workers and the public."* > *(Source: [LLNL NIF Target Chamber](https://lasers.llnl.gov/about/how-nif-works/target-chamber))*

### Conclusion for Reactor Design:
This highlights that the generated energy **has never been fed into the electricity grid**. The NIF operates strictly as a physics laboratory and completely lacks the thermal or electrical engineering infrastructure (such as heat exchangers or steam turbines) required for power generation. Currently, the high-energy 14.1 MeV neutrons are simply absorbed by a boron-injected concrete blanket for radiation shielding purposes, meaning the fusion energy ultimately dissipates as waste heat.
